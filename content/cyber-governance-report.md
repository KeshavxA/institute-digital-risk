# Cyber Governance Report: Q3 Update

Effective cyber governance is no longer just an IT issue; it is a critical board-level mandate. Our latest Q3 report highlights the shifting responsibilities of executives in the face of mounting regulatory pressure.

## Regulatory Landscape

New directives introduced across the UK and EU mandate stricter reporting timelines for cyber incidents. Failure to comply now carries significant financial penalties and, in some jurisdictions, personal liability for corporate officers.

### Board Responsibilities
- **Oversight:** Boards must actively oversee cyber risk management strategies.
- **Expertise:** At least one board member should possess verifiable cybersecurity expertise.
- **Reporting:** Clear lines of communication must be established between the CISO and the board.

## The Role of the CISO

The CISO's role has transitioned from a purely technical manager to a strategic business leader. They are tasked with translating complex technical risks into business impacts that the board can understand and act upon.

> Effective governance bridges the gap between the server room and the boardroom.

By aligning cyber risk management with overall business objectives, organizations can turn a defensive necessity into a competitive advantage.
